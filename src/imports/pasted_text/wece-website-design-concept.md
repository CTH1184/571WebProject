Design a modern, welcoming, high-fidelity website concept for “Women in Electrical & Computer Engineering (WECE)” at UW–Madison.

This is a student organization website for women and underrepresented genders in electrical and computer engineering. The design should feel professional, supportive, community-oriented, and polished enough to present as a real student org website. Do not code the site—create a complete website design system and page mockups in Figma style.

Overall style:
- Clean, modern university organization aesthetic
- Friendly and inclusive, but still professional and credible
- UW–Madison-inspired visual direction with cardinal red accents, white backgrounds, soft neutrals, and limited blush/pink accents inspired by the WECE logo
- Strong accessibility and readability
- Clear information hierarchy
- Mobile-responsive layouts should be considered
- Use rounded cards, subtle shadows, strong spacing, and modern typography
- Feel more visually engaging than a basic university template
- Blend the professionalism of an engineering org with the warmth of a community-focused women’s organization

Create a full design concept with these public pages:
1. Home
2. About
3. Board Members
4. Events
5. Sponsors
6. Contact
7. Get Involved

Also include a lightweight concept for:
8. Board Member Account / Admin Dashboard

Primary goals of the website:
- Introduce WECE and its mission
- Help new students understand what the organization does
- Highlight leadership and events
- Showcase sponsors and build sponsor credibility
- Encourage students to get involved
- Make it easy for future board members to update content without touching code

Include a consistent sitewide design system:
- Sticky top navigation
- Footer with contact info and social links
- Primary and secondary button styles
- Card components
- Section headers
- Form fields and dropdowns
- Calendar embed section styling
- Instagram feed styling
- Sponsor logo grid/carousel styling
- Board member profile cards
- Dashboard widgets for admins

Homepage requirements:
- Hero section with a strong headline about empowering women in ECE at UW–Madison
- Short mission-focused intro
- CTA buttons such as “Get Involved,” “View Events,” and “Meet the Board”
- Featured sections previewing About, Events, Sponsors, and Get Involved
- A visual “Why WECE?” or “What We Offer” section with 3–4 cards such as community, professional development, mentorship, and industry connections
- Upcoming events preview
- Sponsor logo strip
- Instagram preview section
- Friendly, high-quality imagery placeholders showing students, engineering, collaboration, and campus/community
- A polished footer with email, Instagram, LinkedIn, and sponsor/member contact pathways

About page requirements:
- Background on the organization
- Mission statement
- Who can join
- Organizational values
- Timeline or story section about the club
- Section explaining impact: networking, mentorship, learning, support, professional development
- FAQ-style section for new students
- Warm but credible tone

Board Members page requirements:
- Grid of profile cards with headshots
- Name, role, short bio, focus area, and optional LinkedIn/email icons
- Featured executive board section
- Ability to highlight positions like President, VP Operations, VP Internals, VP Externals, VP Finance, and director roles
- Clean and easy to update layout

Events page requirements:
- Featured upcoming events section
- Embedded Google Calendar placeholder/module
- Past event photo gallery or highlights
- Instagram feed or recent social content section
- Event categories such as social, professional, academic, mentorship, and industry
- CTA for members to RSVP, subscribe, or get updates

Sponsors page requirements:
- Professional sponsor-facing layout
- Current and past sponsors displayed by tier
- Clean logo grid
- Short copy explaining why sponsor WECE
- Benefits of sponsorship section
- CTA for companies to contact the organization
- A polished, credible tone suitable for industry partners

Contact page requirements:
- Contact cards for general inquiries, new members, and sponsors
- Contact form with name, email, reason for contact, and message
- Social links
- Optional FAQ or “best way to reach us” section
- Friendly but professional layout

Get Involved page requirements:
- Strong CTA for prospective members
- Interactive interest form section
- Options for joining mailing list, attending events, mentorship, leadership, and sponsorship interest
- “What to expect” section for new members
- Steps to join
- FAQ for first-time visitors
- Make this page especially inviting and action-oriented

Admin / Board Account dashboard concept:
Create a simple content-management style dashboard for board members so they can update the site without coding.
Include:
- Login screen concept
- Dashboard home
- Editable modules/cards for events, board members, sponsors, homepage announcements, and contact information
- “Add event,” “edit board profile,” “upload sponsor logo,” and “update homepage banner” actions
- Simple, intuitive, student-friendly interface
- Show how non-technical board members could maintain the site

Design deliverables to generate:
- High-fidelity homepage mockup
- High-fidelity mockups for all listed public pages
- At least one mobile layout example for the homepage and one interior page
- A mini design system section with colors, type styles, buttons, cards, and form inputs
- Admin dashboard concept screens

Content direction:
- Keep copy realistic for a UW student organization
- Tone should be encouraging, welcoming, ambitious, and professional
- Avoid generic corporate language
- Make the site feel active, student-led, and community-centered
- Prioritize usability, clarity, and visual polish

Visual inspiration:
- Use strong homepage storytelling
- Make the homepage feel more custom, editorial, and image-driven than a standard university site
- Include social proof, event visibility, and a stronger sense of student community
- Keep the design feasible for eventual implementation in a no-code or AI-assisted website builder

Output:
Create a cohesive website prototype with clear page layouts, reusable components, and a realistic student-organization brand identity for WECE at UW–Madison.
